## 📗 CHAPTER 2: BUSINESS PROCESSES & DECISION MAKING

| Topic | Definition | Use Case | Real-Life Example |
|-------|-----------|----------|------------------|
| **Business Process** | A set of related activities that produce a specific output for a customer | Any end-to-end organizational activity with defined input and output | Amazon's order-to-delivery process: Order placed → Payment → Warehouse pick → Pack → Ship → Deliver |
| **Operational Process** | Core processes that directly create and deliver value to customers | Manufacturing, sales, service delivery | Zomato's food delivery process — directly serves the customer |
| **Management Process** | Governs and controls operational processes | Strategic planning, performance management, budgeting | Tata Group's annual strategic planning process |
| **Supporting Process** | Enables primary processes without creating direct customer value | HR, Finance, IT support, Legal | Infosys's HR recruitment process — supports delivery teams |
| **BPR** | Fundamental rethinking and radical redesign of processes for dramatic improvement | When a process is too slow, costly, or broken | Ford reduced AP headcount 500→125 by eliminating invoice document |
| **Structured Decision** | Routine, repetitive decision with clear rules — can be automated | Daily operational decisions | Auto-reorder when inventory < 100 units |
| **Semi-structured Decision** | Partly routine + partly requires judgment | Monthly/quarterly management decisions | Bank deciding to approve a ₹50L business loan |
| **Unstructured Decision** | Novel, complex — no standard procedure, requires full human judgment | Strategic decisions | CEO deciding whether to acquire a competitor |
| **Simon's Intelligence Phase** | Scanning for problems and opportunities | Identifying that customer churn has increased 20% | Zomato's analytics flags that order frequency dropped in Delhi |
| **Simon's Design Phase** | Developing alternative solutions to the identified problem | Brainstorming options to reduce churn | "Offer discounts / improve delivery time / add new restaurants" |
| **Simon's Choice Phase** | Selecting the best alternative from designed options | Making the final decision | Zomato decides to offer 30-minute delivery guarantee |
| **Value of Information — Relevance** | Information must relate directly to the decision at hand | Sales data only useful for sales decisions, not HR decisions | Regional manager gets city-wise sales data — not national average |
| **Value of Information — Timeliness** | Information must be available when needed; stale info = no value | Stock market data must be real-time | Yesterday's share price is useless for today's trading decision |

## 📗 CHAPTER 3: INFORMATION SYSTEMS OVERVIEW

| Topic | Definition | Use Case | Real-Life Example |
|-------|-----------|----------|------------------|
| **Data** | Raw, unprocessed facts with no meaning on their own | Input to IS for processing | "47, 83, 21, 66" — just numbers, no context |
| **Information** | Data that has been processed and organized to give meaning | Output of IS used for decisions | "MBA Batch 2024 average = 71.4 marks; top scorer = Ravi (83)" |
| **Knowledge** | Patterns and insights derived from information over time | Used for strategy and expertise | "Sales always spike in October due to festive season" |
| **GIGO** | Garbage In, Garbage Out — IS output quality depends on input data quality | Data quality management | If customer age is entered as "225" → all age-based analytics are wrong |
| **Information System (IS)** | Organized combination of people, hardware, software, networks, procedures, and data to collect, process, and distribute information | Any organized information processing activity | Zomato's IS: app (software) + servers (hardware) + delivery data (data) + delivery partners (people) |
| **SDLC Approach** | Waterfall-style sequential IS development (Plan→Analyze→Design→Build→Test→Maintain) | Large, well-defined IS projects with stable requirements | Government ERP implementation — fixed scope, 2-year project |
| **Prototyping Approach** | Build quick IS model, get feedback, refine repeatedly | When user requirements are unclear | Startup builds MVP, shows to users, iterates based on feedback |
| **End-User Development** | Non-IT professionals build their own IS applications | Quick custom tools without IT department | Finance manager builds sales tracker in Excel with VBA macros |
| **RAD** | Rapid Application Development — fast delivery using parallel teams and timeboxing | When speed of delivery is critical | New digital banking feature built in 8 weeks using RAD methodology |

## 📗 CHAPTER 4: COMPUTER-BASED IS TYPES

| Topic | Definition | Use Case | Real-Life Example |
|-------|-----------|----------|------------------|
| **OAS** | Office Automation System — automates routine office tasks | Day-to-day communication, document management, scheduling | Infosys uses MS Teams + SharePoint to manage 3 lakh employees globally |
| **TPS** | Transaction Processing System — records, processes, and stores daily business transactions | High-volume routine transaction recording | UPI payment: verifies balance → deducts → credits → generates receipt — all in <2 seconds |
| **Batch Processing (TPS)** | Transactions collected and processed together at scheduled intervals | When real-time processing not needed | Monthly payroll: all attendance data collected → salaries processed on last day of month |
| **Real-Time Processing (TPS)** | Each transaction processed immediately | When instant update is critical | ATM cash withdrawal — balance updated instantly |
| **MIS** | Collects data from TPS and provides structured periodic reports to middle managers | Regular management reporting | Big Bazaar regional manager receives weekly store-wise sales performance report |
| **DSS** | Interactive IS supporting semi-structured decisions by combining data + analytical models | Complex "what-if" analysis | IndiGo's revenue management DSS: optimizes ticket prices based on demand, time-to-departure, competitor prices |
| **GDSS** | Supports collective decision making by groups with anonymous voting and e-brainstorming | Committee or board-level group decisions | Multinational board meeting using Mentimeter for anonymous voting on 5 acquisition targets |
| **EIS/ESS** | IS designed for top executives — highly summarized visual dashboards with drill-down | Strategic overview for C-suite | CEO's dashboard shows live revenue, market share, stock price across all divisions — drill into any metric |
| **Expert System** | Computer program that emulates a human expert's decision-making using rules (IF-THEN logic) | Replacing human expertise in specific domain | Bank credit scoring: IF income > 5L AND credit score > 750 AND no defaults THEN approve loan |
| **Inference Engine** | The "reasoning" component of expert system — applies rules from knowledge base to reach conclusions | Processing rules to generate recommendations | Medical diagnosis ES: applies 200 rules to symptoms to diagnose condition |
| **Knowledge Base** | Repository of facts and expert rules in an Expert System | Storing domain expertise digitally | Medical ES knowledge base: 10,000 disease-symptom-treatment rules from 20 expert physicians |
| **EUC** | End-User Computing — non-IT professionals directly develop and use their own IS applications | Reducing dependency on IT department backlog | Marketing manager builds campaign tracker in Airtable without writing any code |
| **Shadow IT** | Unauthorized IS applications created by users outside IT governance | Risk of EUC — no security or compliance oversight | Sales team uses personal Dropbox to share customer data — bypasses company security |

## 📗 CHAPTER 5: IT & STRATEGIC ADVANTAGE

| Topic | Definition | Use Case | Real-Life Example |
|-------|-----------|----------|------------------|
| **Strategic Advantage** | Sustained ability to outperform competitors in ways valuable to customers and hard to imitate | Competitive positioning | Jio's digital-first strategy — ₹0 calls — destroyed competition |
| **Porter's Value Chain** | Series of activities a company performs to create value — each activity can be enhanced by IT | IT investment prioritization across all functions | Amazon: IS enhances inbound logistics (supplier portals) + operations (warehouse robots) + outbound (GPS tracking) + marketing (AI recommendations) + service (chatbots) |
| **Porter's Five Forces** | Framework of 5 competitive forces — IT can be used to counter each | Analyzing IT's competitive role | Netflix's vast IS infrastructure → high barrier for new entrants |
| **Cost Leadership via IT** | Using IS to reduce costs below competitors while maintaining acceptable quality | Price-sensitive markets | D-Mart's lean IS → minimal waste → lowest grocery prices in India |
| **Differentiation via IT** | Using IS to create unique product/service features that justify premium price | Value-added competition | Apple's IS ecosystem (iCloud, App Store, AirDrop) creates unique experience |
| **IT-Enabled Services (ITES)** | Services delivered using IT as the primary enabler, often remotely | Outsourcing services globally | TCS, Infosys provide IT services to US/European companies — India earns $200B+ annually |
| **Seamless Organization** | Organization where IT dissolves boundaries between departments and external partners | Enterprise integration via ERP + APIs | HUL: Salesperson enters Jaipur order → automatically updates Pune factory + Mumbai finance + distributor |
| **Virtual Corporation** | Temporary network of independent companies connected by IT, each contributing core competency | Project-based business model | Apple designs in California + Foxconn manufactures in China + carriers sell globally — all coordinated by IS |
| **Intranet** | Private internal internet accessible only to employees | Internal communication and knowledge sharing | HUL's employee portal — policies, HR forms, training materials |
| **Extranet** | Secure internet accessible to specific external partners | B2B collaboration with suppliers/dealers | Maruti's dealer management IS — dealers check inventory, place orders |
| **Outsourcing** | Contracting non-core functions to external specialized firms | Reducing cost or accessing expertise | Wipro, TCS provide IT services to US banks — labor cost 80% lower than US |
| **Offshore Outsourcing** | Outsourcing to a firm in a distant country for cost advantage | IT and BPO outsourcing to India | JP Morgan outsources IT development to TCS India |
| **KPO** | Knowledge Process Outsourcing — high-value knowledge work outsourced | Research, analytics, legal services | US law firm outsources legal research and document review to Indian firm |

## 📗 CHAPTER 6: INTERNATIONAL IS

| Topic | Definition | Use Case | Real-Life Example |
|-------|-----------|----------|------------------|
| **International IS (IIS)** | IS that crosses national boundaries to support global business operations | Multinational company coordination | Infosys's global project management IS — teams in India, US, Europe share same project data |
| **Centralized IIS** | All IS controlled from HQ; subsidiaries use HQ's system | When consistency and control are priority | Traditional MNC with one global ERP, country offices just enter data |
| **Decentralized IIS** | Each country builds its own IS independently | When local responsiveness matters most | Holding company where each subsidiary is fully autonomous |
| **Federated IIS** | Some IS centralized (global), some local (country-specific) | Best of both — balance control and flexibility | Global HR system (centralized) + local payroll processing (local) |
| **Transnational IIS** | Fully integrated — information flows freely in all directions | Large MNCs needing global efficiency AND local flexibility | Unilever's SAP S/4HANA deployment across 100+ countries |
| **Inter-Organizational IS (IOS)** | IS that automates information flow across organizational boundaries — connecting two or more organizations | Supply chain integration between companies | Maruti's IS automatically sends purchase orders to Tata Steel when steel inventory falls below threshold |
| **EDI** | Electronic Data Interchange — computer-to-computer exchange of standard business documents without human intervention | Automating B2B transactions | Walmart sends thousands of POs to P&G automatically via EDI every day — zero manual effort |
| **VMI** | Vendor Managed Inventory — supplier manages buyer's inventory directly using shared IS data | Reducing buyer's inventory management burden | P&G monitors Walmart's P&G product stock levels — auto-replenishes before shelves empty |
| **CPFR** | Collaborative Planning, Forecasting, and Replenishment — partners jointly plan and forecast | Reducing bullwhip effect | Big retailers and FMCG companies share 12-month demand forecasts to plan production together |

## 📗 CHAPTER 7: STRUCTURED SYSTEM ANALYSIS & SDLC

| Topic | Definition | Use Case | Real-Life Example |
|-------|-----------|----------|------------------|
| **Structured System Analysis** | Systematic approach to analyzing existing systems and defining new IS requirements using graphical tools | Before any IS development begins | Before building new banking app, analysts document all existing processes using DFDs |
| **Feasibility Study (TELOS)** | Evaluating viability of proposed IS from technical, economic, legal, operational, and schedule perspectives | Go/no-go decision for IS project | Bank evaluates: Can we build mobile app? (Technical) Will savings exceed cost? (Economic) Does it comply with RBI? (Legal) |
| **DFD — Data Flow Diagram** | Graphical representation of how data flows through a system — showing external entities, processes, data stores, and flows | Documenting system requirements before development | Amazon order processing DFD: Customer → Process Order → Order DB → Fulfillment → Customer |
| **Context Diagram (DFD Level 0)** | Highest-level DFD showing entire system as one process and all external entities | First step in DFD creation — big picture view | Online banking system as one circle; external entities: Customer, Bank, RBI |
| **Data Dictionary** | Repository of metadata — defines all data elements, structures, flows and stores in the system | Ensuring consistent data definitions across development team | Customer_ID: Numeric, 10 digits, mandatory, unique, auto-generated |
| **ERD** | Entity Relationship Diagram — graphical model of entities and their relationships for database design | Database design | E-commerce ERD: Customer places Order → Order contains Product |
| **Logical Design** | Specifies WHAT the system will do — technology-independent | Requirements to design transition | "System will store customer orders with date, items, total amount" |
| **Physical Design** | Specifies HOW the system will be built — technology-specific | Actual technical blueprint | "Orders stored in MySQL table: order_id INT PK, customer_id INT FK, total DECIMAL(10,2)" |
| **Waterfall Model** | Linear, sequential SDLC model — each phase completed before next begins | Well-defined, stable requirements projects | Government ERP: fixed scope, multi-year, all requirements known upfront |
| **Prototype Model** | Build quick prototype, get user feedback, refine repeatedly | Unclear requirements, UI-heavy projects | Startup builds quick app mockup, shows to 10 users, redesigns based on feedback |
| **Spiral Model** | Risk-driven iterative model — each cycle includes risk analysis | Large, complex, high-risk projects | Defense information system — risk of failure is catastrophic |
| **Agile Model** | Short sprints (2-4 weeks), continuous delivery, adapts to change | Dynamic requirements, digital products | Swiggy's app team delivers new features every 2 weeks via Agile sprints |
| **Parallel Running** | Old and new IS run simultaneously before full cutover | Critical systems where failure risk must be zero | SBI runs old CBS alongside new system for 3 months before switching |
| **Pilot Conversion** | Deploy new IS in one location first, expand after validation | Large national rollouts | IRCTC deploys new booking system in one zone first, then all India |
| **UAT** | User Acceptance Testing — actual end users test system to confirm it meets needs | Final validation before go-live | Bank employees test new loan processing system before it goes live for customers |

## 📗 CHAPTER 8: ERP, CRM & SCM

| Topic | Definition | Use Case | Real-Life Example |
|-------|-----------|----------|------------------|
| **ERP** | Integrated software connecting ALL core business functions in a single system with shared database | Organization-wide process integration | SAP at Tata Steel: one system manages procurement, production, HR, finance, sales simultaneously |
| **ERP Integration Benefit** | All departments share ONE database — no data silos, real-time visibility | Eliminating data inconsistency and duplication | When Maruti makes a sale, finance, inventory, and logistics data update automatically in SAP |
| **CRM** | Business strategy + technology managing all customer interactions across lifecycle to build long-term relationships | Customer acquisition, retention, and growth | Jio uses CRM to track every subscriber's usage, complaints, plan history — enables personalized retention offers |
| **Operational CRM** | Automates customer-facing processes — sales force automation, marketing automation, service automation | Sales and service process management | Salesforce: sales rep tracks all leads, contacts, proposals, and deals in one system |
| **Analytical CRM** | Analyzes customer data to discover patterns and predict behavior | Customer intelligence and targeting | Zomato AI predicts which customers will churn → sends targeted "come back" offers before they actually leave |
| **Collaborative CRM** | Connects all customer touchpoints — ensures same customer history visible across all channels | Omnichannel customer experience | Amazon customer service: agent sees all orders, returns, chats, emails from every channel in one screen |
| **SCM** | Management of goods, services, information, and finance from raw material to final customer using IS | End-to-end supply chain optimization | Amul: IS coordinates 3.6M milk farmers → processing plants → distributors → retailers across India |
| **Bullwhip Effect** | Small demand fluctuations amplify as they move upstream — consumer's ±5% swing becomes supplier's ±80% swing | Understanding supply chain instability | During COVID, consumer hoarding of sanitizer (small demand spike) caused manufacturers to over-order chemicals globally |
| **VMI (Supply Chain)** | Supplier directly manages buyer's inventory using shared IS data | Reducing buyer's inventory cost and stockouts | P&G monitors Walmart shelf data → auto-ships Tide detergent when stock falls below threshold — no PO needed |
| **JIT (Just-In-Time)** | Producing/receiving goods exactly when needed — minimizing inventory | Manufacturing and procurement optimization | Toyota receives car parts from suppliers 2 hours before needed — near-zero warehouse inventory |
| **RFID in SCM** | Radio Frequency Identification tags track products in real time without human scanning | Warehouse and logistics tracking | Amazon warehouse: RFID on every package → system knows exact location of 10M+ items at all times |

## 📗 CHAPTER 9: INFORMATION INFRASTRUCTURE

| Topic | Definition | Use Case | Real-Life Example |
|-------|-----------|----------|------------------|
| **Information Infrastructure** | Underlying foundation of IT resources (hardware, software, networks, data, people) supporting all IS | Organizational IT planning and investment | Amazon's AWS infrastructure: millions of servers, petabytes of storage, global networks — enables entire Amazon business |
| **IT Architecture** | Blueprint defining how organization's IT components are organized and integrated | IT strategic planning | Reliance Jio's network architecture: nationwide 4G/5G infrastructure serving 450M subscribers |
| **Cloud Computing** | Delivering IT resources over internet on pay-per-use basis | Replacing on-premises IT investment | Startups use AWS instead of buying servers — pay ₹10K/month instead of ₹1Cr investment |
| **IaaS** | Infrastructure as a Service — provides servers, storage, networking via cloud | When company needs computing power without hardware investment | Flipkart uses AWS EC2 servers — scales up 10x during Big Billion Days, scales down after |
| **PaaS** | Platform as a Service — provides IaaS + OS + development platform | When developers need a ready platform to build apps | Developers build apps on Google App Engine without managing servers or OS |
| **SaaS** | Software as a Service — full application delivered via browser | When company needs software without IT infrastructure | Zoho CRM, Salesforce, Gmail — use via browser, vendor manages everything |
| **Public Cloud** | Shared infrastructure owned by cloud provider, accessible to all | Cost-effective for non-sensitive workloads | Any company using AWS, Azure, or Google Cloud |
| **Private Cloud** | Dedicated cloud for one organization | Sensitive data that can't be on shared infrastructure | SBI's own cloud data center for banking transactions |
| **Hybrid Cloud** | Mix of public and private cloud | Some data sensitive, some not | Hospital: patient records on private cloud; website and email on public cloud |
| **Edge Computing** | Processing data near the source (device/sensor) rather than central cloud | Real-time applications where latency matters | Factory IoT sensors process temperature data locally → only alerts sent to cloud |
| **DPDPA 2023** | Digital Personal Data Protection Act — India's law for protecting citizens' personal data | Compliance for any company handling Indian user data | Zomato must get explicit consent before using customer location data for marketing |
| **IT Act 2000** | India's primary law governing cybercrime, digital signatures, and e-contracts | Legal framework for digital business | Hacking a company's server is a criminal offense under IT Act Section 66 |
| **NII** | National Information Infrastructure — nationwide network of telecom, computing, and IS resources enabling digital economy | National digital development | BharatNet: connecting 2.5 lakh panchayats with optical fiber for rural broadband |
| **BharatNet** | Government initiative connecting rural India with optical fiber broadband | Rural digital inclusion | Village panchayat in Bihar accesses government services online through BharatNet connectivity |
| **UPI/NPCI** | Unified Payments Interface — IS infrastructure enabling instant digital payments across all banks | National payment infrastructure | 10 billion+ UPI transactions/month — India leads world in real-time digital payments |

## 📗 CHAPTER 10: STRATEGIC IS & TECHNOLOGY

| Topic | Definition | Use Case | Real-Life Example |
|-------|-----------|----------|------------------|
| **Strategic IS (SIS)** | IS that aligns with long-term strategy to create sustainable competitive advantage | When IT transforms competitive position, not just automates | Amazon's logistics IS — not just efficiency, it created a new industry standard (Prime delivery) |
| **CIO** | Chief Information Officer — aligns IS strategy with business strategy, manages IS function | Senior IT leadership in organizations | TCS CIO oversees all internal IS systems + aligns IT with TCS's delivery strategy |
| **CTO** | Chief Technology Officer — manages technology infrastructure, R&D, emerging tech adoption | Technology innovation and architecture | Zomato's CTO oversees app architecture, infrastructure, and new technology bets |
| **McFarlan's Strategic Quadrant** | Classifies IS by current strategic importance and future potential (Strategic/Turnaround/Factory/Support) | IS portfolio investment prioritization | Strategic quadrant: Amazon's recommendation AI — critical now and in future → invest heavily |
| **McFarlan — Factory** | IS critical now but low future strategic potential — keep running reliably | Maintaining essential but commoditized IS | ATM network — essential for banks but no longer gives competitive advantage |
| **McFarlan — Turnaround** | IS low strategic importance now but HIGH future potential — invest to develop | Bet on future competitive IS | Bank building AI chatbot before competitors — low value today, high future value |
| **IS Strategic Alignment** | IS strategy must align with business strategy, organizational processes, and IT capabilities | Ensuring IT investments support business goals | Flipkart's IS strategy: maximize delivery speed → supports business strategy of "fastest e-commerce" |
| **BPR (Business Process Re-engineering)** | Fundamental rethinking and radical redesign of processes for dramatic improvement using IT as enabler | When processes are fundamentally broken or inefficient | Income Tax Department: paper returns → e-filing → AI scrutiny → refunds in weeks instead of months |
| **Network Effect** | IS/platform becomes more valuable as more people use it | Platform business model strategy | WhatsApp: 500M Indian users → each new user makes it MORE valuable for all existing users |
| **Switching Cost via IT** | When IS integration makes it costly/difficult for customers to switch to competitor | Customer retention strategy | Microsoft Office ecosystem — all files in .docx, .xlsx formats → switching to Google Docs is painful |
| **IT in Banking** | Core banking, mobile payments, AI fraud detection, algorithmic trading | Financial services modernization | HDFC's fraud detection AI: flags suspicious transaction within milliseconds, before money leaves account |
| **IT in Retail** | E-commerce, dynamic pricing, demand forecasting, last-mile logistics IS | Retail competitiveness | Flipkart changes product prices 10,000+ times per day using real-time demand and competitor pricing AI |
| **IT in Agriculture** | Precision farming, crop disease AI, market IS, supply chain | Modernizing agriculture | eNAM (National Agriculture Market) IS: farmers sell crops to buyers across India without middlemen |
| **IT in Healthcare** | EHR, telemedicine, AI diagnostics, hospital management IS | Healthcare quality and accessibility | Practo app: patient books appointment → doctor sees full history → prescription generated digitally |
