## 2. 🎯 APPLICATIONS OF MARKETING RESEARCH

> **Mnemonic: "PCAMP-DC-BSA"**
> *(Already met in Lesson 1 scope — now with real-world depth)*

---

### A. 🛍️ PRODUCT RESEARCH

> Research related to **developing, testing, and modifying products and services**

**Key Activities:**
1. **Concept Testing** — Test if a new product idea appeals to consumers before development
   - *Example: Tata Motors tests "electric scooter for women" concept before investing*
2. **Product Development Research** — Identify gaps, desired features, ideal specifications
3. **Product Testing / Blind Testing** — Respondents test product without knowing brand
   - *Example: Pepsi Challenge — blind taste test vs Coke*
4. **Brand Name Testing** — Which name resonates best with target consumers?
5. **Packaging Research** — Which design, colour, and size is preferred on shelf?
6. **Product Life Cycle Research** — Track product performance across stages (Introduction → Decline)
7. **Test Marketing** — Launch product in one city before national rollout
   - *Example: Maggi tested in Pune before national launch*

---

### B. 💰 PRICING RESEARCH

> Research to determine **what price consumers will accept** and what maximizes profit

**Key Activities:**
1. **Price Sensitivity Research** — How does demand change with price?
2. **Price Elasticity Studies** — Is the product price-elastic or inelastic?
3. **Psychological Pricing Research** — ₹99 vs ₹100 effect on purchase decision
4. **Competitive Pricing Analysis** — What are rivals charging?
5. **Conjoint Analysis** — How much is each product attribute worth to consumers?
   - *Example: How much extra will consumers pay for "organic" label?*
6. **Price-Quality Relationship** — Do consumers associate higher price with better quality?

---

### C. 📢 PROMOTION / ADVERTISING RESEARCH

> Research to create, test, and evaluate **advertising and promotional effectiveness**

**Key Activities:**
1. **Copy Testing (Pre-testing)** — Test ad before airing — will it communicate correctly?
2. **Media Research** — Which media (TV, digital, print) reaches target audience?
   - Tools: BARC (TV ratings), IRS (print readership)
3. **Message Research** — Which message/slogan resonates most?
4. **Recall and Recognition Tests** — After viewing, can consumers recall the ad?
   - **Aided Recall:** "Do you remember seeing an ad for Surf Excel?"
   - **Unaided Recall:** "Name any detergent ads you remember"
5. **Post-Campaign Evaluation** — Did the campaign achieve its objectives?
6. **Sales Promotion Effectiveness** — Did the "Buy 2 Get 1" offer increase sales volume?

---

### D. 🏪 DISTRIBUTION / PLACE RESEARCH

> Research on **channel effectiveness, retail performance, and supply chain**

**Key Activities:**
1. **Channel Preference Research** — Where do consumers prefer to buy? Online vs offline?
2. **Retail Audit** — Measure product availability, shelf placement, competitor stock
   - *Example: Nielsen Retail Audit for FMCG companies*
3. **Store Location Research** — Which location maximizes footfall and sales?
4. **Mystery Shopping** — Researcher poses as customer to evaluate service quality
5. **Supply Chain Research** — Identify bottlenecks and inefficiencies in delivery

---

### E. 🙋 CONSUMER / BUYER BEHAVIOUR RESEARCH

> Deep understanding of **who buys, what they buy, why, when, and how**

**Key Activities:**
1. **Consumer Attitude Studies** — Measure attitudes toward brands, products, companies
2. **Motivation Research** — Uncover deep psychological drivers using projective techniques
3. **Lifestyle Research (Psychographics)** — How do consumers live, think, and aspire?
4. **Customer Satisfaction Studies (CSAT)** — Post-purchase satisfaction measurement
5. **Net Promoter Score (NPS)** — "Would you recommend this brand?" (0-10 scale)
6. **Customer Journey Mapping** — Track all touchpoints from awareness to purchase
7. **Brand Loyalty Research** — What drives repeat purchase? What causes switching?

---

### F. 📊 MARKET ANALYSIS RESEARCH

> Understanding the **market itself** — its size, growth, and competitive landscape

**Key Activities:**
1. **Market Size Estimation** — How large is the total addressable market (TAM)?
2. **Market Share Analysis** — What % of the market does each brand hold?
3. **Demand Forecasting** — What will sales look like in the next 1-3 years?
4. **Market Segmentation Research** — Divide market into meaningful consumer groups
5. **Competitor Analysis** — SWOT analysis of rivals, benchmarking
6. **New Market Entry Research** — Is it viable to enter a new city/country/segment?

---

### G. 💼 CORPORATE / SOCIAL RESEARCH

1. **Corporate Image Research** — How is the company perceived by public and stakeholders?
2. **Employee Satisfaction Research** — Internal HR research for workforce insights
3. **Social Media Listening** — Track brand mentions, sentiment online (Netnography)
4. **Political Research** — Voter opinion polls, exit polls, constituency profiling

## 3. 📏 ATTITUDE MEASUREMENT & SCALING — APPLIED CONTEXT

*(Deep dive already in Lesson 2 — this section shows WHERE they are applied)*

### What is an Attitude?

> **Attitude** = A person's enduring **evaluation, feeling, and action tendency** toward an object (brand, product, person, issue)

### Three Components of Attitude — **"ABC Model"**:

| Component | Meaning | Example (Towards Tesla) |
|-----------|---------|------------------------|
| **A — Affective** | Feelings/Emotions | "I love Tesla — it's so cool!" |
| **B — Behavioural** | Action tendency | "I plan to buy a Tesla next year" |
| **C — Cognitive** | Beliefs/Knowledge | "Tesla has the best EV range in the world" |

> **Mnemonic: "ABC" — Affective, Behavioural, Cognitive**

### Attitude Scales in Practice:

| Scale | Real-World Application |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Likert Scale** | Customer satisfaction surveys (1-5 stars), NPS, employee engagement |
| **Semantic Differential** | Brand image studies — "How do you perceive Flipkart?" |
| **Stapel Scale** | Quick attribute rating in telephone surveys |
| **Thurstone Scale** | Academic/policy research on social attitudes |

### Attitude vs Behaviour Gap:

> **Problem:** Stated attitudes don't always predict actual behaviour!
> - "I'll definitely buy organic food" → actually buys regular food because it's cheaper
> - Solution: Use **behavioural measures** (actual purchase data, loyalty cards) alongside attitude scales

## 4. 🇮🇳 CHALLENGES IN CONDUCTING MARKETING RESEARCH IN INDIA

> This is a **very India-specific and frequently asked** section — examiners love this!

> **Mnemonic: "DILL CAPS"** *(Like Dilli — Delhi!)*
> - **D**iversity of population
> - **I**lliteracy and low education levels
> - **L**anguage barriers
> - **L**ow respondent cooperation
> - **C**ost constraints (high cost, low budgets)
> - **A**ccessibility issues (rural areas, infrastructure)
> - **P**oor secondary data quality
> - **S**ocial and cultural sensitivities

---

### Detailed Challenges:

#### 1. 🌈 Diversity of Population
- India has **1.4 billion people** across 28 states, diverse religions, castes, income levels
- A single national sample must capture this diversity — very complex
- What works in Mumbai may not work in rural Bihar
- **Solution:** Region-specific sampling, local language questionnaires

#### 2. 📚 Low Literacy Rates
- Large rural population has **limited literacy** — can't fill written questionnaires
- **Solution:** Oral interviews, picture-based scales, mobile surveys with audio

#### 3. 🗣️ Language Barriers
- India has **22 official languages** and hundreds of dialects
- Questionnaire must be translated accurately — mistranslation = invalid data
- **Solution:** Back-translation (translate to Hindi → translate back to English → compare)

#### 4. 🤐 Low Respondent Cooperation
- Indians are traditionally **reluctant to share opinions** with strangers
- Privacy concerns, suspicion of data misuse
- Sensitive topics (income, caste, sexuality) get refusals
- **Solution:** Build rapport, explain purpose, guarantee anonymity, offer incentives

#### 5. 💸 Cost Constraints
- MR in India is **expensive** relative to organizational budgets (especially SMEs)
- Wide geography → high fieldwork costs
- **Solution:** Online surveys, syndicated data, panel research

#### 6. 🏘️ Accessibility Issues — Rural India
- **67% of Indians live in rural areas** — hard to reach physically
- Poor road connectivity, no internet access in remote areas
- **Solution:** Mobile-based surveys, village-level interviewers, partner with NGOs

#### 7. 📊 Poor Secondary Data Quality
- Government data is often **outdated** (last Census = 2011!)
- Trade data may be underreported (grey markets, cash economy)
- Company data is often not publicly disclosed
- **Solution:** Primary research, syndicated commercial data

#### 8. 🎭 Social and Cultural Sensitivities
- **Social desirability bias** is very high in India — people say what sounds good
- Joint family culture → purchasing decisions involve multiple people
- Caste, religion, gender roles influence responses
- **Solution:** Indirect questioning, projective techniques, use same-gender interviewers

#### 9. 🌐 Digital Divide
- Online research excludes **rural, elderly, poor, and less educated** segments
- Only 55% of India has active internet usage
- **Solution:** Mixed mode — online + face-to-face + telephone

#### 10. ⚠️ Interviewer Quality
- Trained, neutral interviewers are hard to find in smaller cities/towns
- Risk of interviewer bias, fabricated data ("armchair surveys")
- **Solution:** Supervision, back-checks, GPS tracking of field visits

## 5. ⚖️ ETHICAL ISSUES IN MARKETING RESEARCH

> **Definition:** Ethical issues in MR are situations where the **rights, welfare, and dignity** of research participants, clients, or society may be compromised.

> **Mnemonic: "PICAD"** — *"Principal Investigators Create Accountable Data"*
> - **P**rivacy of respondents
> - **I**nformed consent
> - **C**onfidentiality
> - **A**nonymity
> - **D**eception issues

---

### Ethical Responsibilities — Three Parties:

```
Ethical Issues in MR
│
├── A. Researcher's Ethics (towards respondents)
├── B. Researcher's Ethics (towards clients)
└── C. Client's Ethics (towards researchers & public)
```

---

### A. ⚖️ RESEARCHER'S ETHICAL DUTIES TOWARDS RESPONDENTS

| Ethical Issue | What It Means | Violation Example |
|--------------|---------------|------------------|
| **Informed Consent** | Respondents must know they're being studied and agree | Secretly recording conversations |
| **Right to Privacy** | Cannot invade personal space or use data without permission | Accessing phone data without consent |
| **Right to Withdraw** | Participants can stop at any time without penalty | Pressuring respondents to complete survey |
| **Anonymity** | Respondent's identity not revealed in any report | Publishing names alongside responses |
| **Confidentiality** | Data not shared with unauthorized parties | Selling respondent data to third parties |
| **No Deception** | Cannot mislead respondents about purpose | Telling respondents it's a "health survey" when it's actually advertising research |
| **No Harm** | Research must not cause psychological, social, or physical harm | Asking trauma survivors to relive painful experiences without support |
| **Honest Incentives** | Cannot bribe or manipulate respondents | Promising gifts then not delivering |

---

### B. ⚖️ RESEARCHER'S ETHICAL DUTIES TOWARDS CLIENTS

| Ethical Issue | What It Means |
|--------------|---------------|
| **Objectivity** | Report findings truthfully — even if results are negative for the client |
| **Confidentiality** | Client's research briefs, strategies, and findings must not be leaked |
| **No Fictitious Data** | Never fabricate or manipulate data ("armchair surveys") |
| **Competence** | Only take on projects the researcher is qualified to handle |
| **Transparency** | Clearly disclose methodology, limitations, and margin of error |
| **No Conflict of Interest** | Don't simultaneously work for competing brands |

---

### C. ⚖️ CLIENT'S ETHICAL DUTIES

| Ethical Issue | What It Means |
|--------------|---------------|
| **Honest Briefing** | Give researcher complete and accurate background — don't hide relevant info |
| **Misuse of Data** | Don't use research data beyond agreed-upon purpose |
| **Misrepresentation** | Don't selectively quote findings out of context to mislead public |
| **Sugging / Frugging** | Never use MR as a disguise for selling (Sugging = Selling Under Guise of research; Frugging = Fundraising Under Guise) |
| **Publication Bias** | Don't suppress unfavorable findings — only publish positive results |

> [!WARNING]
> **SUGGING and FRUGGING are major ethical violations** — Know these terms! Very commonly asked.
> - **SUGGING:** "Selling Under the Guise" of research — salesperson pretends to be doing a survey but actually trying to sell
> - **FRUGGING:** "Fundraising Under the Guise" of research

---

### D. 🔒 DATA PRIVACY AND LEGAL COMPLIANCE

- **Data Protection Laws:** Research must comply with data privacy regulations
  - India: **Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA), 2023**
  - Global: **GDPR** (General Data Protection Regulation) — Europe
- **Data Storage:** Respondent data must be securely stored and deleted after use
- **Children's Research:** Special ethical safeguards when researching minors — parental consent mandatory

---

### E. 🌍 SOCIETAL / BROADER ETHICS

| Issue | Concern |
|-------|---------|
| **Research on Sensitive Groups** | Elderly, children, disabled, poor — require extra safeguards |
| **Environmental Impact** | Large-scale primary research has carbon footprint |
| **Cultural Sensitivity** | Research must respect cultural norms and beliefs |
| **Misuse for Propaganda** | Research findings must not be distorted for political/corporate manipulation |

---

### Code of Ethics — Key Organizations:

| Organization | Role |
|-------------|------|
| **MRSI (Market Research Society of India)** | Sets ethical standards for MR in India |
| **ESOMAR** | European society of opinion and marketing research — global ethics code |
| **AMA (American Marketing Association)** | Ethical code for marketing and research professionals |
| **ICC/ESOMAR International Code** | Global framework for responsible research practice |
