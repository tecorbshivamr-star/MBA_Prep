## 📌 CHAPTER 1: Introduction to Macroeconomics

### Core Idea
Macro studies the **economy as a whole** — GDP, inflation, unemployment, interest rates.

### Must-Know Comparisons
| | Macro | Micro |
|---|---|---|
| Scope | Entire economy | Individual firms/consumers |
| Measures | GDP, CPI, unemployment | Price, demand, supply |

### Circular Flow
- **Closed economy**: GDP = National Income = Total Expenditure
- **Open economy**: Leakages (S+T+M) = Injections (I+G+X)

### Key Relationships
| Relationship | Direction | Law/Curve |
|---|---|---|
| GDP ↑ → Unemployment ↓ | Inverse | **Okun's Law** |
| Unemployment ↓ → Inflation ↑ | Inverse | **Phillips Curve** |
| Interest Rate ↑ → Investment ↓ | Inverse | Investment Function |
| G ↑ → GDP ↑ | Direct | **Multiplier Effect** |

### Budget
```
Surplus: T > G | Balanced: T = G | Deficit: T < G
```

### Business Cycle: Expansion → Peak → Contraction → Trough → Recovery
- **Recession** = 2 consecutive quarters of negative GDP growth
- **Leading**: Stock market | **Coincident**: GDP | **Lagging**: Unemployment

### Key Formulas
```
GDP = C + I + G + (X−M)
Real GDP = (Nominal GDP / GDP Deflator) × 100
Inflation Rate = [(CPI₁−CPI₀)/CPI₀] × 100
Multiplier = 1/(1−MPC)
```

## 📌 CHAPTER 2: National Accounts & GDP Measurement

### 3 Approaches to GDP (All Give Same Answer)
| Approach | Formula |
|---|---|
| **Expenditure** | C + I + G + (X−M) |
| **Income** | Wages + Rent + Interest + Profit + Depreciation + Net Indirect Taxes |
| **Value Added** | Σ (Output − Intermediate Inputs) — avoids double counting |

### National Income Hierarchy (MUST KNOW)
```
GDP (Market Price)
  + NFIA → GNP
  − Depreciation → NNP (Market Price)
  − Indirect Taxes + Subsidies → National Income (NI)
  − Corp. Taxes − Retained Earnings + Transfers → Personal Income (PI)
  − Personal Taxes → Disposable Income (DI)
```

### What's EXCLUDED from GDP
❌ Intermediate goods | ❌ Second-hand sales | ❌ Transfers | ❌ Household work | ❌ Black economy

### GDP at MP vs FC
```
GDP at MP = GDP at FC + Indirect Taxes − Subsidies
```

### India's GDP Problems
1. Large **informal sector** (~unrecorded)
2. **Black economy** (~20-25% of GDP)
3. **Non-market activities** (household work)
4. **Data collection** challenges
5. **Free digital services** (zero price)
6. **Subsistence agriculture** hard to value

### Green GDP
```
Green GDP = GDP − Environmental Costs − Resource Depletion
```
HDI = Better welfare measure (Income + Education + Health)

## 📌 CHAPTER 3: Economic Growth (Solow Model)

### Rule of 70
```
Years to double = 70 / Growth Rate (%)
India at 7% → doubles every 10 years
```

### Production Function
```
Y = A × K^α × L^(1-α)      [Cobb-Douglas]
y = A × k^α                 [Per worker form]
α ≈ 1/3 (Capital share), (1-α) ≈ 2/3 (Labor share)
```

### Solow Model — Key Equation
```
Δk = s×f(k) − (δ+n+g)×k
Investment  − Break-even investment
```

### Steady State: `s×f(k*) = (δ+n+g)×k*`

| Parameter ↑ | Effect on k* | Effect on y* |
|---|---|---|
| Savings rate (s) | ↑ Higher | ↑ Higher |
| Depreciation (δ) | ↓ Lower | ↓ Lower |
| Population growth (n) | ↓ Lower | ↓ Lower |
| TFP (A) | ↑ Higher | ↑ Higher |

### Balanced Growth Path
- Total Y & K grow at **n+g**
- Per-worker Y grows at **g** (TFP growth)

### TFP (Growth Accounting)
```
ΔY/Y = ΔA/A + α(ΔK/K) + (1−α)(ΔL/L)
TFP Growth = GDP Growth − Capital's contribution − Labor's contribution
```

### KEY LESSON
> **Only TFP (technological progress) sustains long-run per capita growth.**
> Higher savings → Higher level of output, NOT permanently higher growth rate.

### Convergence
- **Absolute**: All countries converge to same SS — weak evidence
- **Conditional**: Countries converge to their OWN SS — strong evidence
- Poor countries grow faster (transition dynamics) — but only if institutions are good

## 📌 CHAPTER 4: IS-LM Model

### What Each Curve Means
| Curve | Market | Slope | Equation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **IS** | Goods Market | ↘ Downward | Y = A − b×r |
| **LM** | Money Market | ↗ Upward | r = (k/h)Y − Ms/(h×P) |

### IS Shifts RIGHT when: ↑G, ↓T, ↑Confidence, ↑Exports, ↓Imports
### LM Shifts RIGHT when: ↑Money Supply, ↓Price Level

### Equilibrium: IS = LM → gives (Y*, r*)

### Special Cases
| Case | LM Shape | Fiscal Policy | Monetary Policy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Normal** | Upward slope | Partially effective | Partially effective |
| **Liquidity Trap** | Horizontal (r=0) | ✅ FULLY effective | ❌ INEFFECTIVE |
| **Classical** | Vertical | ❌ INEFFECTIVE (full crowding out) | ✅ FULLY effective |

### Crowding Out
```
↑G → ↑r → ↓Private Investment (partial offset)
Full crowding out: Vertical LM → ΔG = −ΔI exactly
Zero crowding out: Horizontal LM (Liquidity Trap)
```

### Key Formulas
```
Multiplier = 1/(1−MPC)
Tax Multiplier = −MPC/(1−MPC)
Balanced Budget Multiplier = 1
```

## 📌 CHAPTER 5: AD/AS Framework

### Three Curves
| Curve | Slope | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **AD** | ↘ Downward | Wealth + Interest Rate + Exchange Rate effects |
| **SRAS** | ↗ Upward | Sticky wages; ↑P → ↑Profits → ↑Production |
| **LRAS** | Vertical at Y* | Prices fully flexible; output = potential GDP |

### AD Shifts RIGHT: ↑G, ↓T, ↑Ms, ↑Confidence, ↑Exports
### SRAS Shifts RIGHT: ↓Input prices, ↓Wages, ↑Technology, Good weather
### LRAS Shifts RIGHT: ↑Capital, ↑Labor, ↑TFP, ↑Human capital

### Gaps
```
Inflationary Gap:  Actual Y > Y* → ↑Inflation pressure
Recessionary Gap:  Actual Y < Y* → ↑Unemployment
```

### Demand Shock (SR → LR)
- Positive demand shock → SR: ↑Y, ↑P → LR self-correct: wages rise → SRAS left → Y returns to Y*, P even higher
- Negative demand shock → SR: ↓Y, ↓P → LR: wages fall → SRAS right → Y returns to Y*

### STAGFLATION ⚠️ (Most Important Supply Concept)
```
Negative Supply Shock (↑oil prices) → SRAS shifts LEFT → ↑P AND ↓Y simultaneously
→ Policymaker's nightmare: Can't fix both with demand policy!
```

### Supply-Side Economics
- Laffer Curve: Optimal tax rate maximizes revenue; cutting from too-high rate can ↑revenue
- India's supply-side reforms: GST, PLI schemes, IBC, privatization

## 📌 CHAPTER 6: Phillips Curve, Monetary & Fiscal Policy

### Phillips Curve (Original — A.W. Phillips, 1958)
```
Inverse trade-off: ↓Unemployment ↔ ↑Inflation
Broke down in 1970s (Stagflation destroyed the relationship)
```

### Expectation-Augmented Phillips Curve (Friedman-Phelps)
```
π = πᵉ − β(u − u*)

π = actual inflation
πᵉ = expected inflation
u* = NAIRU (Natural Rate of Unemployment)
```
- **SRPC**: Inverse relationship (exists for given πᵉ)
- **LRPC**: VERTICAL at u* — no long-run trade-off!
- SRPC shifts UP when inflationary expectations rise

### Accelerationist Hypothesis
> Trying to keep u below u* permanently causes ever-accelerating inflation

### Neutrality of Money
```
MV = PY  (Quantity Theory)
Short Run: Money NOT neutral (sticky prices → ↑Ms → ↑Y)
Long Run:  Money IS neutral (↑Ms → only ↑P, no ↑Y)
```

### RBI — Key Numbers
| Rate | Value |
|---|---|
| Repo Rate | 6.50% |
| CRR | 4.5% |
| SLR | 18% |
| Inflation Target | 4% (±2%) |

### RBI's 7 Functions
1. Monetary Authority (MPC — meets every 2 months)
2. Currency Issuer (notes only; ₹1 coin = Govt.)
3. Banker to Government
4. Banker to Banks (Lender of Last Resort)
5. Regulator of Financial System
6. Foreign Exchange Manager (~$640bn reserves)
7. Development Role (UPI, financial inclusion)

### Crowding Out (Recap)
```
↑G → ↑r → ↓Private Investment
More crowding out: Steep LM, Flat IS
Less crowding out: Flat LM (Liquidity Trap), Steep IS
```

### Liquidity Trap (Recap)
- r at zero → ↑Ms ineffective (money hoarded)
- Fiscal policy fully effective (no crowding out)
- Solutions: QE, Negative rates, Forward guidance

## 📌 CHAPTER 7: Theories of Trade & Balance of Payments

### Trade Theories — Quick Reference
| Theory | Core Idea | Key Thinker |
|---|---|---|
| **Mercantilism** | Export max, import min; wealth = gold | 16th–18th C. |
| **Absolute Advantage** | Produce what you're absolutely better at | Adam Smith |
| **Comparative Advantage** | Produce what has lowest opportunity cost — ALL gain! | Ricardo |
| **H-O Model** | Export goods using your abundant factor | Heckscher-Ohlin |
| **Product Life Cycle** | Trade patterns shift as product matures | Vernon |
| **New Trade Theory** | Economies of scale + variety → similar countries trade | Krugman |
| **Porter's Diamond** | 4 factors: Factor conditions, Demand, Related industries, Rivalry | Porter |

### Comparative Advantage — Numerical Steps
1. Calculate OC of each good in each country
2. Country with lower OC for a good has Comparative Advantage in it
3. Both countries gain by specializing and trading

### BOP Structure
```
BOP = Current Account + Capital Account + Financial Account = 0 (always!)
```

| Account | Key Items |
|---|---|
| **Current Account** | Goods trade + Services + Remittances + Factor income |
| **Capital Account** | Capital transfers, non-produced assets |
| **Financial Account** | FDI + FPI + Loans + Forex Reserve changes |

### India's BOP Position
- Goods: Large **DEFICIT** (~$240bn) — oil + gold + electronics
- Services: Large **SURPLUS** (~$140bn) — IT/BPO
- Remittances: Large **SURPLUS** (~$120bn) — world #1
- Current Account: Moderate **DEFICIT** (~1-2% GDP) — manageable
- Sustainable CAD: **< 3% of GDP**

### BOP Correction Methods
Depreciation | Deflation | Trade controls | Borrowing | Attract FDI/FPI

## 📌 CHAPTER 8: Exchange Rates & Mundell-Fleming Model

### Exchange Rate Basics
```
Appreciation = Stronger currency (fewer ₹ per $)
Depreciation = Weaker currency (more ₹ per $)
Real Exchange Rate q = e × (P*/P)
Relative PPP: %Δe = π_domestic − π_foreign
IRP: i = i* + expected depreciation
```

### Exchange Rate Regimes
| Regime | Description | India? |
|---|---|---|
| Fixed | Govt. pegs rate; RBI defends with reserves | No |
| Floating | Market-determined | No |
| **Managed Float** | Market + selective RBI intervention | ✅ **YES** |

### Capital Account Convertibility — India
- **Current Account**: ✅ Fully convertible (since 1994)
- **Capital Account**: ⚠️ Partial (LRS limit $250k/year for individuals)
- **Tarapore Committee**: Recommended gradual CAC with preconditions (low inflation, fiscal deficit, strong banks)

### ⭐ Mundell-Fleming — THE KEY TABLE ⭐

| Policy | Floating Rate | Fixed Rate |
|---|---|---|
| **Fiscal Policy (↑G)** | ❌ **INEFFECTIVE** | ✅ **EFFECTIVE** |
| **Monetary Policy (↑Ms)** | ✅ **EFFECTIVE** | ❌ **INEFFECTIVE** |

### Why?
**Floating + Fiscal**: ↑G → ↑r → Capital IN → Currency APPRECIATES → ↓NX → cancels ↑G
**Floating + Monetary**: ↑Ms → ↓r → Capital OUT → Currency DEPRECIATES → ↑NX → boosts Y
**Fixed + Fiscal**: ↑G → ↑r → Capital IN → Appreciation pressure → RBI buys $ sells ₹ → ↑Ms → ↑Y
**Fixed + Monetary**: ↑Ms → ↓r → Capital OUT → Depreciation pressure → RBI sells $ buys ₹ → ↓Ms → back to start

### ⭐ Impossible Trinity (Trilemma) ⭐

> **Cannot have ALL THREE simultaneously:**
> 1. Fixed Exchange Rate
> 2. Free Capital Mobility
> 3. Independent Monetary Policy
>
> **Choose only 2!**

| Country | Chose | Gave Up |
|---|---|---|
| Eurozone | Fixed rate + Free capital | Monetary independence |
| China (historic) | Fixed rate + Monetary independence | Free capital |
| India/USA/UK | Free capital + Monetary independence | Perfect fixed rate |

## 🎯 TOP 10 EXAM POINTS — Don't Forget!

1. **Circular Flow**: GDP = NI = Expenditure in closed economy
2. **Value Added Method**: Avoids double counting — add only value added at each stage
3. **Solow**: Higher savings → Higher LEVEL not higher GROWTH RATE in LR
4. **IS-LM**: Liquidity Trap → Fiscal works, Monetary fails. Classical → Monetary works, Fiscal fails
5. **AD-AS**: Stagflation = ↑P + ↓Y from supply shock — demand policy can't fix both
6. **LRPC**: Vertical at u* — No long-run trade-off between inflation and unemployment
7. **Neutrality of Money**: Short run NOT neutral; Long run IS neutral (only P changes, not Y)
8. **BOP identity**: CA + Capital A/C + Financial A/C = 0 ALWAYS
9. **Mundell-Fleming**: Floating → Monetary works; Fixed → Fiscal works
10. **Impossible Trinity**: Can NEVER have fixed rate + free capital + monetary independence together
