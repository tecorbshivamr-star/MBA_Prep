## 🟢 BEGINNER LEVEL — "What is this about?"

In physics, a **Lever** allows you to lift a massive boulder using only a small amount of force. 
In finance, **Leverage** does the exact same thing: it uses fixed costs to multiply your profits. 

There are two types of "levers" a company can pull:
1. **Operating Leverage:** Using fixed operating costs (like a factory lease) instead of variable costs. If you sell a lot, your profits explode because rent stays the same!
2. **Financial Leverage:** Using fixed financial costs (interest on a bank loan) instead of equity. If the business booms, you only pay the bank their flat 10% interest, and the shareholders get to keep *all* the remaining massive profits!

**The Catch:** Levers work in both directions. If sales drop, those fixed rent and interest payments don't disappear. You will go bankrupt much faster. Leverage multiplies profits AND losses.

## 🟡 INTERMEDIATE LEVEL — "Key Concepts & Frameworks"

### 1. Degree of Operating Leverage (DOL)
Measures how sensitive Operating Profit (EBIT) is to a change in Sales.
> **DOL = Contribution / EBIT**
*(Where Contribution = Sales - Variable Costs, and EBIT = Contribution - Fixed Costs).*
If DOL is 3, it means a 10% increase in Sales will cause a 30% increase in EBIT.

### 2. Degree of Financial Leverage (DFL)
Measures how sensitive Earnings Per Share (EPS) is to a change in EBIT.
> **DFL = EBIT / (EBIT - Interest)**
If DFL is 2, a 10% increase in EBIT will cause a 20% increase in EPS.

### 3. Degree of Combined Leverage (DCL)
Shows the total risk of the firm (Operating Risk + Financial Risk).
> **DCL = DOL × DFL = Contribution / (EBIT - Interest)**
If DCL is 6, a 10% increase in Sales will cause a 60% increase in EPS. 

### 4. EBIT-EPS Analysis
This is how management decides between Plan A (all equity) and Plan B (debt + equity). You calculate the EPS for both plans at different levels of expected EBIT to see which plan makes the shareholders richer.

## 🔴 ADVANCED / EXAM LEVEL — "How to write it in the exam?"

### Q. What is Operating and Financial Leverage? Calculate DOL, DFL, and DCL for a company given the following data: Sales = ₹50,00,000; Variable Cost = 60% of Sales; Fixed Costs = ₹10,00,000; Interest = ₹4,00,000. (15 Marks)

**1. Definition of Leverage:**
Leverage refers to the employment of an asset or source of funds for which the firm pays a fixed cost or fixed return. It magnifies the returns to the equity shareholders, but proportionately increases the firm's risk profile. 

*   **Operating Leverage:** Relates to the left side of the balance sheet (asset structure). It is the firm's ability to use fixed operating costs to magnify the effects of changes in sales on its earnings before interest and taxes (EBIT). High fixed costs (like airlines) mean high operating leverage.
*   **Financial Leverage:** Relates to the right side of the balance sheet (capital structure). It is the firm's ability to use fixed financial charges (debt interest or preference dividends) to magnify the effects of changes in EBIT on its earnings per share (EPS). High debt means high financial leverage.

**2. Step-by-Step Calculation:**

**Step 1: Prepare the Income Statement**
*   **Sales:** ₹50,00,000
*   **Less: Variable Costs** (60% of 50L): ₹30,00,000
*   **= Contribution:** ₹20,00,000
*   **Less: Fixed Costs:** ₹10,00,000
*   **= EBIT (Operating Profit):** ₹10,00,000
*   **Less: Interest:** ₹4,00,000
*   **= EBT (Earnings Before Tax):** ₹6,00,000

**Step 2: Calculate Degree of Operating Leverage (DOL)**
*   Formula: DOL = Contribution / EBIT
*   DOL = 20,00,000 / 10,00,000
*   **DOL = 2.0**
*   *(Interpretation: A 1% increase in sales will result in a 2% increase in EBIT).*

**Step 3: Calculate Degree of Financial Leverage (DFL)**
*   Formula: DFL = EBIT / (EBIT - Interest) = EBIT / EBT
*   DFL = 10,00,000 / 6,00,000
*   **DFL = 1.67**
*   *(Interpretation: A 1% increase in EBIT will result in a 1.67% increase in EPS).*

**Step 4: Calculate Degree of Combined Leverage (DCL)**
*   Formula: DCL = DOL × DFL  *(or Contribution / EBT)*
*   DCL = 2.0 × 1.67
*   **DCL = 3.34**
*   *(Interpretation: A 1% increase in sales will result in a 3.34% increase in EPS. This quantifies the total risk of the firm).*
