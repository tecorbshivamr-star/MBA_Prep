## 🟢 BEGINNER LEVEL — "What is this about?"

Imagine you are managing a pizza restaurant. 
Before the month begins, you set a **Standard**: "Every pizza should take exactly 200 grams of cheese, and cheese should cost exactly ₹500 per kg."
At the end of the month, you check the actual bills and find out you used 250 grams per pizza, and the cheese cost ₹600 per kg!

This difference between your *planned* cost and your *actual* cost is called a **Variance**. 
**Standard Costing** is the system of setting these targets, measuring the variances, and then yelling at the right manager (the Purchasing Manager for the price, the Head Chef for the extra 50 grams!).

---

## 🟡 INTERMEDIATE LEVEL — "Key Concepts & Frameworks"

There are two main buckets of Variances you need to learn: Material and Labour.

### 1. Material Variances
- **Material Cost Variance (MCV):** The total difference in money. (Standard Cost - Actual Cost).
- **Material Price Variance (MPV):** Did the Purchasing Manager pay too much per kg?
- **Material Usage Variance (MUV):** Did the factory workers waste raw materials on the floor?
> *Check your math: MCV = MPV + MUV*

### 2. Labour Variances
- **Labour Cost Variance (LCV):** The total difference in wage bills. 
- **Labour Rate Variance (LRV):** Did HR pay the workers a higher hourly rate than planned?
- **Labour Efficiency Variance (LEV):** Did the workers take 5 hours to do a job that should have taken 3 hours? (Lazy workers).
> *Check your math: LCV = LRV + LEV*

### 3. Favourable vs Adverse
- If Actual Cost < Standard Cost = **Favourable (F)**. You saved money!
- If Actual Cost > Standard Cost = **Adverse (A)**. You lost money!

## 🔴 ADVANCED / EXAM LEVEL — "How to write it in the exam?"

### Q. Define Standard Costing. The standard material cost for producing 100 units of Product X is 400 kg at ₹10 per kg. The actual production was 100 units, which consumed 430 kg of material at ₹9 per kg. Calculate the Material Cost, Price, and Usage Variances. (10 Marks)

**1. Definition of Standard Costing:**
Standard Costing is a rigorous management control technique where predetermined estimates of costs (standards) are established based on scientific analysis of materials, labour, and overheads. Actual costs incurred are continuously compared against these standards to calculate variances, thereby enabling "Management by Exception."

**2. Data Extraction:**
*   **Standard Quantity (SQ)** for actual output (100 units) = 400 kg
*   **Standard Price (SP)** = ₹10 per kg
*   **Actual Quantity (AQ)** = 430 kg
*   **Actual Price (AP)** = ₹9 per kg

**3. Step-by-Step Calculation:**

**A. Material Cost Variance (MCV):**
*   *Formula:* (Standard Qty × Standard Price) - (Actual Qty × Actual Price)
*   *MCV* = (400 × 10) - (430 × 9)
*   *MCV* = 4,000 - 3,870
*   **MCV = ₹130 (Favourable / F)**
*   *(Total material cost was ₹130 lower than budgeted).*

**B. Material Price Variance (MPV):**
*   *Formula:* Actual Qty × (Standard Price - Actual Price)
*   *MPV* = 430 × (10 - 9)
*   *MPV* = 430 × (+1)
*   **MPV = ₹430 (Favourable / F)**
*   *(The purchasing manager negotiated a lower price, saving ₹1 per kg on all 430 kg purchased).*

**C. Material Usage Variance (MUV):**
*   *Formula:* Standard Price × (Standard Qty - Actual Qty)
*   *MUV* = 10 × (400 - 430)
*   *MUV* = 10 × (-30)
*   **MUV = ₹300 (Adverse / A)**
*   *(The production floor was inefficient and wasted 30 extra kg of material, costing the company ₹300).*

**4. Verification (The Math Check):**
*   MCV = MPV + MUV
*   +130 (F) = +430 (F) - 300 (A)
*   **+130 = +130 (Verified!)**

**5. Management Action:**
The overall variance is favourable, but looking deeper reveals a problem. The purchasing manager did an excellent job securing cheaper material (₹430 F), but this cheaper material might have been of lower quality, causing the factory workers to waste more of it (₹300 A). Management must investigate the quality of the raw material.
