## 9.2 Introduction

> Traditional management accounting focused **only on financial results** — profit, cost, ROI. But financial figures alone **cannot tell the full story** of a business.

**Problems with purely financial measurement:**
- Only looks **backward** (historical data)
- Ignores **customer satisfaction, employee skills, innovation**
- Encourages **short-term thinking** (cut costs today, hurt long-term)
- Doesn't capture **intangible assets** (brand, knowledge, relationships)

> 🧠 **BugZero Example:**
> BugZero's finance report shows ₹30L profit this year. Looks great!
> But: 3 top clients left. 2 senior QAs quit. No new skills learned.
> → The financial report MISSED the real warning signs!
> **A balanced performance system would have caught these early.**

## 9.3 Performance Measurement ⭐

> **Performance Measurement** = The process of **quantifying the efficiency and effectiveness** of past actions of an organization against its strategic objectives.

### 📌 Types of Performance Measures

| Type | Description | Examples |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Financial Measures** | Expressed in monetary terms | Profit, ROI, Revenue, Cost variance |
| **Non-Financial Measures** | Expressed in non-monetary terms | Customer satisfaction %, defect rate, employee turnover |
| **Quantitative** | Can be measured in numbers | Units produced, hours worked |
| **Qualitative** | Subjective/descriptive | Brand reputation, culture |

---

### 📌 Traditional Financial Performance Measures

#### 1. Return on Investment (ROI) ⭐

```
ROI = (Net Profit / Capital Employed) × 100

Higher ROI = Better performance
```

**Example:** BugZero earned ₹6L profit on ₹30L capital employed.
```
ROI = (6,00,000 / 30,00,000) × 100 = 20%
```

**Limitations of ROI:**
- Encourages **short-termism** (avoid good projects that reduce ROI temporarily)
- Can be **manipulated** by reducing capital employed
- Doesn't account for **risk** or **cost of capital**

---

#### 2. Residual Income (RI) ⭐

> **RI** = Profit earned **over and above** the minimum required return on capital employed.

```
RI = Net Profit − (Capital Employed × Required Rate of Return)

Positive RI → Value is being created ✅
Negative RI → Not earning enough return ❌
```

**Example:** BugZero profit = ₹6L. Capital = ₹30L. Required return = 15%.
```
Required Return = 30,00,000 × 15% = ₹4,50,000
RI = ₹6,00,000 − ₹4,50,000 = ₹1,50,000 (Positive ✅)
```

---

#### ⚖️ ROI vs Residual Income

| Feature | ROI | Residual Income |
|---------|-----|----------------|
| **Form** | % (ratio) | Absolute ₹ amount |
| **Comparison** | Easy to compare divisions | Hard to compare differently-sized divisions |
| **Decision bias** | May reject good projects (lowers %) | Accepts all projects that exceed required return |
| **Goal congruence** | May conflict with company goals | Better aligned with company goals |

## 9.4 Balanced Scorecard (BSC) ⭐⭐⭐

> **Balanced Scorecard** = A **strategic performance management framework** developed by **Robert Kaplan and David Norton (1992)** that measures organizational performance from **4 perspectives** — balancing **financial and non-financial** measures.

### 📌 Core Idea
```
"What you measure is what you get."
— Traditional accounting measures only financials.
— BSC says: measure FOUR dimensions for a complete picture.
```

### 📌 The 4 Perspectives of BSC ⭐⭐⭐

```
                    ┌─────────────────────────────────┐
                    │         VISION & STRATEGY        │
                    └────────────────┬────────────────┘
          ┌─────────────────────┬────┴────┬──────────────────────┐
          ▼                     ▼         ▼                      ▼
  ┌──────────────┐   ┌──────────────┐  ┌────────────────┐  ┌────────────────┐
  │  FINANCIAL   │   │  CUSTOMER    │  │INTERNAL PROCESS│  │LEARNING&GROWTH │
  │ Perspective  │   │ Perspective  │  │  Perspective   │  │  Perspective   │
  │              │   │              │  │                │  │                │
  │"How do we    │   │"How do our   │  │"What must we   │  │"Can we improve │
  │look to       │   │customers     │  │excel at        │  │& create value?"│
  │shareholders?"│   │see us?"      │  │internally?"    │  │                │
  └──────────────┘   └──────────────┘  └────────────────┘  └────────────────┘
```

---

### 🔵 Perspective 1: Financial ⭐

> How does the company **look to its shareholders/owners**? Is it creating financial value?

**Key Questions:**
- Are we profitable?
- Are revenues growing?
- Are we using assets efficiently?

**Common Measures:**

| Measure | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Revenue Growth | % increase in sales |
| Net Profit Margin | Profit / Sales × 100 |
| ROI / ROE | Return on investment/equity |
| Cost Reduction % | How much costs reduced |
| Earnings per Share (EPS) | Profit per equity share |
| Economic Value Added (EVA) | Value created above cost of capital |

> 💼 **BugZero Financial KPIs:**
> - Revenue per contract: ₹80,000 average
> - Profit margin: 25%
> - Revenue growth: 30% year-on-year

---

### 🟢 Perspective 2: Customer ⭐⭐

> How do **customers perceive** the company? Are we meeting their needs and building loyalty?

**Key Questions:**
- Are customers satisfied?
- Are we retaining existing customers?
- Are we acquiring new customers?
- Are we delivering on time?

**Common Measures:**

| Measure | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Customer Satisfaction Score | Survey ratings (e.g., CSAT, NPS) |
| Customer Retention Rate | % of customers who return |
| Market Share | % of total market captured |
| New Customer Acquisition | Number of new clients |
| On-time Delivery Rate | % of deliveries on schedule |
| Customer Complaints | Number of complaints received |

> 💼 **BugZero Customer KPIs:**
> - Client satisfaction score: 4.5/5
> - Repeat client rate: 70%
> - New clients per quarter: 5
> - On-time delivery: 92%

---

### 🟠 Perspective 3: Internal Business Process ⭐⭐

> What **internal processes** must the company **excel at** to satisfy customers and achieve financial goals?

**Key Questions:**
- How efficient is our production/service?
- What is the quality of our output?
- How innovative are we?
- Where are we losing time/money?

**Common Measures:**

| Measure | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Defect Rate / Error Rate | % of defective output |
| Cycle Time | Time to complete one process |
| Productivity | Output per employee/hour |
| Process Efficiency | Output / Input ratio |
| Innovation Rate | No. of new products/services launched |
| Capacity Utilisation | Actual output / Max capacity |

> 💼 **BugZero Internal Process KPIs:**
> - Bug detection rate: 95% (5% escaping to client)
> - Average test cycle time: 3 days (target: 2 days)
> - Automation coverage: 60% of test cases
> - Rework rate: 8% (target: < 5%)

---

### 🔴 Perspective 4: Learning and Growth ⭐⭐

> Can the company **continuously improve** and **build capabilities** for the future? This is the **foundation** of the BSC.

**Key Questions:**
- Are employees skilled and growing?
- Is the company investing in technology?
- Is there a culture of innovation?
- Are we retaining talent?

**Common Measures:**

| Measure | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Employee Satisfaction | Survey scores |
| Employee Retention Rate | % employees staying |
| Training Hours per Employee | Investment in skills |
| Skills/Certifications Gained | New qualifications |
| R&D Spend | Investment in innovation |
| IT System Uptime | Technology reliability |
| Suggestions Implemented | Employee innovation input |

> 💼 **BugZero Learning & Growth KPIs:**
> - QA certifications per year: 3 per engineer
> - Training days per employee: 10/year
> - Employee retention: 85%
> - New automation tools adopted: 2 per year

### ✅ Advantages of BSC

| Advantage | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Balanced view** | Financial + Non-financial = complete picture |
| **Strategic alignment** | Links daily operations to long-term strategy |
| **Forward-looking** | Performance drivers predict future success |
| **Communication tool** | Makes strategy visible to all employees |
| **Goal congruence** | Aligns individual goals with organizational goals |
| **Prevents short-termism** | Non-financial measures protect long-term health |

### ❌ Limitations of BSC

| Limitation | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Complex to implement** | Identifying right measures is difficult |
| **Too many measures** | Risk of information overload |
| **No clear weightings** | Hard to know which perspective matters most |
| **Subjective measures** | Non-financial measures can be hard to quantify |
| **Time-consuming** | Requires significant management effort |
| **Not a strategy setter** | BSC measures strategy but doesn't create it |

## 9.5 Performance Drivers and Weighting ⭐⭐

### 📌 Performance Measures vs Performance Drivers

| Feature | Performance Measures | Performance Drivers |
|---------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Also called** | Lag Indicators | Lead Indicators |
| **Nature** | Outcome — what HAS happened | Cause — what WILL drive results |
| **Timing** | Past/Historical | Future-predictive |
| **Examples** | Profit, Customer Satisfaction score | Training hours, R&D spend |
| **Purpose** | Tell you the result | Tell you how to get better results |

```
CAUSE AND EFFECT:
Training Hours (Driver) → Employee Skill → Quality of Work → Customer Satisfaction → Revenue (Measure)
      (Lead Indicator)                                                    (Lag Indicator)
```

> 🧠 **BugZero Example:**
> **Performance Measure (Lag):** Client satisfaction score = 4.5/5 (tells what happened)
> **Performance Driver (Lead):** QA certification hours per month (if this drops → satisfaction will drop later)

---

### 📌 Weighting Performance Measures

> Since all 4 BSC perspectives may not be **equally important**, organizations assign **weights** based on strategic priorities.

**Example: BugZero's Weighted BSC**

| Perspective | Weight | Score (1-10) | Weighted Score |
|-------------|--------|--------------|----------------|
| Financial | 40% | 7 | 2.8 |
| Customer | 30% | 9 | 2.7 |
| Internal Process | 20% | 6 | 1.2 |
| Learning & Growth | 10% | 8 | 0.8 |
| **Overall Score** | **100%** | | **7.5 / 10** |

> **Why Weight?** A startup like BugZero may weight Customer (35%) higher than Financial (30%) in early stages — because winning customers is priority #1. A mature company may flip the weights.

---

### 📌 Characteristics of Good Performance Measures

```
A good performance measure should be:
✅ SMART — Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
✅ Aligned with strategy
✅ Mix of financial AND non-financial
✅ Mix of lead AND lag indicators
✅ Not too many — focus on KEY measures (KPIs)
✅ Controllable — measurable by the person responsible
```

> **F**inancial → **C**ustomer → **I**nternal Process → **L**earning & Growth
> And the cause-effect runs **bottom-up**: Learning → Internal → Customer → Financial!

> [!NOTE]
> **ROI vs RI for Decision Making:**
> If a division has ROI = 20% and a new project gives 18% ROI:
> → **ROI method**: Reject (lowers division ROI from 20% to somewhere between)
> → **RI method**: Accept if 18% > required return rate (say 15%) → RI is positive = ACCEPT
> **RI gives better decisions = goal congruence with company!**
