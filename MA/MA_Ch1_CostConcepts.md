## 🟢 BEGINNER LEVEL — "What is this about?"

Financial Accounting (reading a Balance Sheet) tells you if the company made a profit last year. It looks to the **past**.
**Management Accounting** looks to the **future**. It helps internal managers make decisions like: "Should we drop this product?", "How many units do we need to sell to break even?", or "At what price should we sell this new laptop?"

To make these decisions, a manager must understand **Costs**.
If you make a pizza, the cheese is a **Variable Cost** (the more pizzas you make, the more cheese you need). But the rent for the pizza shop is a **Fixed Cost** (you pay ₹20,000 rent whether you sell 1 pizza or 1,000 pizzas).

---

## 🟡 INTERMEDIATE LEVEL — "Key Concepts & Frameworks"

### 1. Classification of Costs
- **Fixed Costs:** Do not change with production volume (Rent, Manager's Salary).
- **Variable Costs:** Change proportionally with production (Raw Materials, Direct Labour).
- **Relevant vs Sunk Cost:** A *Relevant Cost* changes your future decision. A *Sunk Cost* is money already spent that you can't get back (e.g., money spent on market research last year). Ignore sunk costs when making decisions!
- **Opportunity Cost:** The benefit lost when choosing one alternative over another. (e.g., If you use your warehouse to store inventory, the opportunity cost is the rent you could have made by renting it out).

### 2. The Cost Sheet
A logical flow of how costs add up:
1. Direct Material + Direct Labour = **Prime Cost**
2. Prime Cost + Factory Overheads = **Factory Cost**
3. Factory Cost + Admin Overheads = **Cost of Production**
4. Cost of Production + Selling Overheads = **Total Cost**

### 3. Marginal Costing (CVP Analysis)
This is the core of Management Accounting. 
> **Contribution = Sales - Variable Costs**
Contribution is the money left over to "contribute" towards paying off your Fixed Costs. Once Fixed Costs are fully paid off, any extra contribution becomes pure Profit.

> **Break-Even Point (BEP) = Fixed Costs / Contribution per unit**
This tells you exactly how many units you need to sell to make zero profit and zero loss.

## 🔴 ADVANCED / EXAM LEVEL — "How to write it in the exam?"

### Q. Distinguish between Marginal Costing and Absorption Costing. A company sells a product for ₹50. Variable cost is ₹30. Fixed cost is ₹1,00,000. Calculate the Break-Even Point (BEP) in units and the Margin of Safety if actual sales are 7,000 units. (15 Marks)

**1. Marginal vs Absorption Costing (Comparison Table):**
| Basis | Marginal Costing | Absorption Costing |
|-------|------------------|--------------------|
| **Treatment of Fixed Costs** | Fixed production costs are treated as **Period Costs** and written off to the P&L entirely in the current period. | Fixed production costs are treated as **Product Costs** and absorbed into the inventory valuation. |
| **Inventory Valuation** | Closing stock is valued at Variable Cost only. | Closing stock is valued at Total Cost (Variable + Fixed). |
| **Profit Measurement** | Profit = Contribution - Fixed Costs. | Profit = Sales - Total Cost. |
| **Utility for Decision Making** | Excellent for short-term decisions (pricing, accept/reject order). | Used for external financial reporting (Ind AS/IFRS compliant). |

**2. Numerical Solution (CVP Analysis):**

**Step 1: Calculate Contribution per unit**
*   Selling Price (SP) = ₹50
*   Variable Cost (VC) = ₹30
*   **Contribution per unit** = SP - VC = 50 - 30 = **₹20**

**Step 2: Calculate Break-Even Point (BEP)**
*   Fixed Costs (FC) = ₹1,00,000
*   **BEP (in units)** = FC / Contribution per unit
*   BEP = 1,00,000 / 20 = **5,000 units**
*   *(Interpretation: The firm must sell 5,000 units just to cover all costs. The 5,001st unit will generate ₹20 of profit).*

**Step 3: Calculate Margin of Safety (MOS)**
*   Actual Sales = 7,000 units
*   **Margin of Safety (in units)** = Actual Sales - BEP Sales
*   MOS = 7,000 - 5,000 = **2,000 units**
*   *(Interpretation: Sales can drop by 2,000 units before the company starts making a loss. This represents the risk buffer).*
