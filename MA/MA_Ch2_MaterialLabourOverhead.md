## 🟢 BEGINNER LEVEL — "What is this about?"

Every physical product has three main cost elements:
1. **Material:** The wood to make a chair.
2. **Labour:** The carpenter who makes the chair.
3. **Overhead:** The electricity bill for the carpenter's workshop.

A management accountant's job is to control these three costs. 
- You don't want to buy too much wood (money stuck in inventory).
- You want to pay the carpenter efficiently (piece rate vs time rate).
- You need a fair way to add a portion of the electricity bill to the cost of *each* chair produced (Overhead Absorption).

---

## 🟡 INTERMEDIATE LEVEL — "Key Concepts & Frameworks"

### 1. Material Control (EOQ)
How much material should you order at once? 
If you order too much, you pay high storage (carrying) costs. If you order too little, you pay high transport (ordering) costs.
> **EOQ (Economic Order Quantity) = √(2AO / C)**
*(A = Annual Demand, O = Ordering Cost per order, C = Carrying Cost per unit per year)*

### 2. Labour Control
- **Time Rate:** Paying ₹100 per hour. (Good for quality work, bad for speed).
- **Piece Rate:** Paying ₹50 per chair made. (Good for speed, bad for quality).
- **Labour Turnover:** The rate at which workers quit. High turnover is terrible because you constantly spend money hiring and training new people.

### 3. Overhead Control (Absorption)
Overheads are indirect costs (like rent or manager's salary). You can't trace rent directly to a single chair. So you "absorb" it.
- **Overhead Absorption Rate (OAR) = Total Budgeted Overheads / Total Budgeted Machine Hours (or Labour Hours).**
- *Example:* If factory rent is ₹1,00,000 and total machine hours are 10,000, then OAR is ₹10 per machine hour. If Chair A takes 2 machine hours to make, you add ₹20 of rent to the cost of Chair A.

## 🔴 ADVANCED / EXAM LEVEL — "How to write it in the exam?"

### Q. Explain the concept of Economic Order Quantity (EOQ). Calculate EOQ and Re-order Level from the following data: Annual consumption = 10,000 units, Cost per order = ₹50, Cost per unit = ₹2, Inventory carrying cost = 10% per annum, Maximum usage = 400 units/week, Maximum lead time = 6 weeks. (10 Marks)

**1. Definition of EOQ:**
Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) is the optimal order size that minimizes the total inventory costs, which comprise Ordering Costs (cost of placing an order, transport) and Carrying Costs (cost of storage, insurance, opportunity cost of capital). At EOQ, Total Ordering Cost exactly equals Total Carrying Cost.

**2. Numerical Solution:**

**Step A: Calculate EOQ**
*   **A (Annual Consumption)** = 10,000 units
*   **O (Ordering Cost)** = ₹50 per order
*   **C (Carrying Cost per unit)** = 10% of ₹2 = ₹0.20 per unit per year.
*   **Formula:** $EOQ = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times A \times O}{C}}$
*   $EOQ = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 10,000 \times 50}{0.20}}$
*   $EOQ = \sqrt{\frac{1,000,000}{0.20}} = \sqrt{5,000,000}$
*   **EOQ = 2,236 units** (approx)
*   *(Interpretation: The company should order 2,236 units every time it places an order to minimize total inventory costs).*

**Step B: Calculate Re-order Level (ROL)**
*   **Concept:** The inventory level at which a new order must be placed to ensure stock arrives before the warehouse is empty.
*   **Formula:** ROL = Maximum Usage × Maximum Lead Time
*   **Maximum Usage** = 400 units per week
*   **Maximum Lead Time** = 6 weeks
*   **ROL** = 400 × 6 = **2,400 units**
*   *(Interpretation: The moment the warehouse inventory drops to exactly 2,400 units, the manager must trigger a new order of 2,236 units).*

### Brief Notes on Labour Premium Plans:
*   **Halsey Plan:** The worker gets a guaranteed time wage PLUS a bonus of 50% of the time saved.
*   **Rowan Plan:** The bonus is a proportion of time saved relative to the standard time (Time Saved / Standard Time × Time Taken × Hourly Rate). Prevents workers from rushing too fast.
