## 🟢 BEGINNER LEVEL — "What is this about?"

If Job Costing (Chapter 3) is for a custom tailor, **Process Costing** is for a Sugar Factory. 
In a sugar factory, you can't tell which exact sugarcane made which exact crystal of sugar. The sugar goes through a continuous process: Crushing → Boiling → Refining. 

Instead of tracking the cost of one bag of sugar, you track the cost of the *entire crushing process* for the month, and then simply divide that total cost by the thousands of bags of sugar produced. 
> **Cost per Unit = Total Process Cost / Total Units Produced**

---

## 🟡 INTERMEDIATE LEVEL — "Key Concepts & Frameworks"

### 1. Normal vs Abnormal Loss
When you process 1,000 kg of sugarcane, you don't get 1,000 kg of sugar. A lot of water evaporates. 
- **Normal Loss:** The expected, unavoidable loss (e.g., 10% evaporation). You ignore this loss and spread its cost over the good units. (The customer pays for normal loss).
- **Abnormal Loss:** If a pipe bursts and you lose an extra 50 kg of juice. This is *preventable*. You calculate the exact cost of this 50 kg and charge it directly to the Profit & Loss Account as a penalty to the manager. (The customer does NOT pay for abnormal loss).
- **Abnormal Gain:** When your actual loss is somehow *less* than your expected normal loss.

### 2. Equivalent Production Units
At the end of the month, you have 500 completed bags of sugar, but you also have 100 bags worth of syrup still boiling in the tanks (Work-In-Progress). How do you divide the costs?
You can't add 500 finished bags + 100 incomplete bags. That's math suicide. 
Instead, you convert the incomplete bags into **Equivalent Units**. If the 100 bags are 50% processed, they are mathematically equivalent to 50 fully completed bags. Total Equivalent Units = 500 + 50 = 550. Now you can divide!

## 🔴 ADVANCED / EXAM LEVEL — "How to write it in the exam?"

### Q. What is Process Costing? Explain the treatment of Normal Loss and Abnormal Loss in a Process Account with the help of a hypothetical example. (15 Marks)

**1. Definition of Process Costing:**
Process costing is an accounting methodology used by companies that mass-produce identical products in a continuous flow (e.g., chemicals, oil refining, cement). Costs are accumulated by department or process for a specified period, and the average cost per unit is computed by dividing total process costs by total equivalent units produced.

**2. Treatment of Losses (The Core Concept):**
*   **Normal Loss:** This is an inherent, unavoidable loss due to the nature of the material or process (e.g., shrinkage, evaporation). 
    *   *Treatment:* The cost of normal loss is absorbed by the good units produced. If the normal loss has any scrap value, it is credited to the Process Account, reducing the total cost.
*   **Abnormal Loss:** This is an unexpected loss caused by inefficiency, machine breakdown, or substandard materials.
    *   *Treatment:* It is separated from the cost of good units so that production efficiency can be accurately judged. It is valued at the cost per unit of normal output and transferred to the Abnormal Loss Account (and ultimately to the Costing P&L Account).

**3. Hypothetical Worked Example:**

**Data for Process 1:**
*   Input introduced: 1,000 units
*   Total Cost of Input (Material + Labour + Overheads): ₹10,000
*   Normal expected loss: 10% of input
*   Scrap value of normal loss: ₹2 per unit
*   Actual output produced: 850 units

**Step 1: Calculate Normal Loss and Normal Output**
*   Normal Loss units = 10% of 1,000 = 100 units
*   Expected Normal Output = 1,000 - 100 = 900 units

**Step 2: Calculate Cost per unit of Normal Output**
*   Cost per unit = (Total Cost - Scrap value of Normal Loss) / Expected Normal Output
*   Cost per unit = (10,000 - (100 units × ₹2)) / 900 units
*   Cost per unit = (10,000 - 200) / 900
*   Cost per unit = 9,800 / 900 = **₹10.89 per unit**

**Step 3: Calculate Abnormal Loss**
*   Expected Output = 900 units. Actual Output = 850 units.
*   Abnormal Loss = 900 - 850 = 50 units.

**Step 4: Valuation for Process Account**
*   Value of Good Output (to Process 2) = 850 units × ₹10.89 = ₹9,256
*   Value of Abnormal Loss (to P&L) = 50 units × ₹10.89 = ₹544
*   *(Note: 9,256 + 544 + 200 scrap = ₹10,000 Total Cost Accounted for).*

**Conclusion:**
By separating abnormal loss and charging it to the P&L, management ensures that the cost per unit of good production remains stable and unaffected by random operational inefficiencies, allowing for accurate pricing and inventory valuation.
